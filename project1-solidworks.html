Description

A 4-cylinder piston system is an internal combustion system that converts energy (from fuel) into energy (motion) using four chambers, or cylinders.

Inside each cylinder, a piston moves up and down in a cycle. The system operates on a four-stroke cycle:

  1. Intake: The piston moves down, pulling a mixture of air and fuel into the cylinder.
  2. Compression: The piston moves upward, compressing the air-fuel mixture.
  3. Power: A spark plug ignites the mixture. The explosion forces the piston back down. This step generates power.
  4. Exhaust: The piston moves back up, pushing the gases out of the cylinder.
